### Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?

DriverPass is a company that aims to provide comprehensive and easily accessible training for DMV driving tests. To achieve this goal, they desired a system that allowed customers to take online practice tests, attend online classes, and make reservations for in-person driving training sessions. The system needed to support both customers and administrative staff.

### What did you do particularly well?

I collected a robust list of functional requirements, which made it easier to model the system accurately.

### If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

I would revise my Activity Diagram for the "Make Reservation" use case. After creating additional diagrams, I realized that one of the steps included in that activity diagram would be better handled as part of a separate use case.

### How did you interpret the user's needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user's needs when designing?

I interpreted the user's needs by translating each client request or feature specification into a functional requirement. From there, I modeled those requirements in a use case diagram, which clarified both what the system needed to do and who would be using it. This approach helped ensure that the system design aligned with the intended users. Considering user needs is essential because a system that does not reflect those needs will ultimately fail to meet its purpose, regardless of how well it is implemented technically.

### How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?

Typically, I begin by determining what a system must do and why. In a formal setting, this corresponds to collecting requirements. Afterward, I consider the processes needed to support those requirements. However, in the future, I intend to rely more heavily on modeling the system before implementation. Doing so will provide a more complete and detailed understanding of system structure and behavior.
